# C3.12 client-side cross-space reactivity — code-verifying synthesis judgment

## Synthesis summary

Four skeptics returned **13 raw findings**; after dedup by root cause (two pairs merged — the two private-`#`-field buildability findings, and the two defect-(i) re-poisoning findings) they resolve to **11 verdicts: 6 serious, 4 minor, 1 note — 11 CONFIRMED, 0 REFUTED.** Three confirmed findings each carried one internal factual error, corrected in the verdicts below: (1) Finding-10's `v2.ts:3519` "doc-set reconcile is same-space-only" is a **misread** — `lane === "space"` is the space-lane-vs-actor-lane axis (see `actingContext` at `v2.ts:2837/2862`), not home-vs-foreign, so a foreign provider reconciles its own space-lane doc-set; the load-bearing support for that finding is `link-resolution.ts:321-322` ("the origin server never pushes other-space docs"), which is solid. (2) Finding-4 labels the watch/pull/delivery machinery "Provider (class 2056)"; it actually lives on **SpaceReplica** (class starts `v2.ts:2420`; `#watchSelectorTracker@2546`, `#updatePromises@2533`, `refreshWatchSet@3448+`, `applyAttributedSessionSync@4985+` are all `>2420`) — both classes are per-space and constructed with only their own space, so the uncomputability conclusion is unaffected. (3) Finding-3's "notifySinks unconditionally when changes exist" omits the `shouldNotifySinks` gate at `v2.ts:4993` — a nuance that actually *strengthens* Finding-1 (the sink-fires-once signal cannot cleanly separate "nothing delivered" from "delivered but not sunk").

**The serious findings cluster on two themes.** Theme 1 — **the client↔server-push boundary is neither probed nor owned** (CR2, CR3): the WO table is entirely client-side, yet the scout's own B1 bundles a client cause (watch torn-down / authority-swallowed / dedup-blocked re-pull) with a server cause (B's origin never pushes third-party commits to the reader's foreign session), and every WO-0 probe sits *inside* the reader's client session, downstream of that boundary. Theme 2 — **named mechanisms and acceptances bind to the wrong seam or the wrong fixture** (CR1, CR4, CR5, CR6): C3.12.1 names a same-space-only latch (`#docPullKicks`) that foreign docs never enter plus an uncomputable `space !== homeSpace` guard; C3.12.3's cited claim/epoch fixtures live in `packages/memory/test/` and never stand up a client `StorageManager` subscription; the defect-(i) exemption already exists but its acceptance is unowned and rests on keying C3.12.2 defers; and the co-hosted **client** reconnect leg is bundled into a flag-flip/doc-closeout row, repeating exactly the C3.10a/C3.10b split (C3A8) and unowned-reconnect-contract (C3A12) the C3 panel already forced.

**Ruling on the root diagnosis (B1, design-gap-not-plumbing).** **B1 is the correct direction over B2, and C3.12 is genuinely a design gap, not plumbing — both CONFIRMED.** A real B replica *is* opened (`cell.ts` → `StorageManager.open(space)` at `v2.ts:1339` → new Provider/SpaceReplica running the standard watch path), so "never subscribed" is false; the direct foreign-cell sink fires exactly once (`cross-space-value-read.test.ts:234-273` asserts only `last?.name === "Ada"`, never mutates B and re-checks); an explicit re-sync no-ops because the pull dedup returns `{ ok: {} }` without re-fetching once a selector is deemed covered (`v2.ts:3291-3292`); and the C3 adversarial review is verifiably server-axis only (a grep for client-reactivity/client-replica/defect-(ii) in `c3-adversarial-review-2026-07-17.md` is empty). B2 (miskeyed reactive dep) is correctly deprioritized for the *direct-sink* path. **But B1 is under-resolved**: it silently spans a client-owned layer and a server-push layer, WO-0's client-internal probes cannot discriminate them (the `shouldNotifySinks` gate at `v2.ts:4993` and the *separate* `scheduler-observations`/`directDirtySeq` cross-space channel at `facade.ts:2192-2216` both muddy "sink fired once ⟹ server delivered nothing"), and no WO owns the server-push outcome. The decomposition's *direction* holds; its *fix-layer resolution* is the binding gap (CR2).

**Ruling on the five owner defaults.**
1. **Standing push vs wake-and-repull — rests on false support.** The default (standing push) may be defensible on latency/reuse grounds, but its stated rationale — wake-and-repull "reintroduces exactly the lifetime-latch staleness class CT-1667 fought" — is **backwards**: CT-1667 staleness comes from *never re-pulling* (the `#docPullKicks` latch treats coverage as liveness, `v2.ts:1031-1037`), and a wake-*driven* re-pull retracts the latch and re-fetches, exactly as FB7 eviction-rekick already does (`handleDocEviction` → `retractDocPullKick`, `v2.ts:1640-1645`). It also understates standing-push's new server surface (CR3).
2. **Refcount + release, gate behind the flag — sound, but the gate is unowned.** The decision is sound; however no WO wires the client-side runtime dial it relies on — the only existing flag (`serverPrimaryExecutionCrossSpaceReadCandidates`) gates server candidate emission, and a grep for any client keep-alive flag is empty (CR10).
3. **Same reactive index, full `(space,id,scope)` key — sound, but load-bearing for defect-(i).** The scheduler is space-agnostic (`facade.ts:2179`), so a correctly-keyed foreign dep works. But this same keying is the input to `schedulerObservationForeignReadSpaces` (`engine.ts:6812-6816`, keyed on `address.space !== ownerSpace`); if the read-log normalizes foreign reads onto home, the defect-(i) exemption silently returns empty and re-poisons the durable floor (CR5).
4. **Lazy read-driven trigger; claim governs suppression not subscription lifecycle — sound, and it is *why* revocation is unowned by the claim path.** The rationale is correct, but decoupling the subscription from the claim is exactly what makes C3.12.3's cited claim/epoch fixtures structurally unable to catch the client-subscription revocation hole (CR4).
5. **Couple to C3.2 epochs, fail closed — sound in intent, but the existing fence is server-side.** `assertForeignAuthorizationEpochsCurrent` (`engine.ts:6717-6748`) is a home-APPLY fence keyed on `provenance.inputBasis` that fences a claimed *commit*, not a client watch — so coupling the *client* subscription to epochs is new client-side machinery, which is precisely the unowned edge CR4 names.

## Verdicts

### [CONFIRMED / serious] V1 — C3.12.1 targets a same-space-only latch and names an uncomputable guard
**WOs:** C3.12.1 (primary); C3.12.2 / C3.12.3 inherit the same phrasing. → **CR1**
**Reasoning:** Both defects verified. `shouldPullDoc`'s *only* call site is `link-resolution.ts:332`, under `reserved = !crossSpace && mgr.shouldPullDoc(...)` (`:331`) inside `if (crossSpace || reserved)` (`:333`) — a cross-space kick fires but never touches `#docPullKicks`; the foreign re-fetch dedup is solely `#watchSelectorTracker` (`v2.ts:2546`, SpaceReplica). `space !== homeSpace` is uncomputable at the named seams: StorageManager (`class @1018`) has **no `#homeSpace` field**, and every `homeSpace` in `v2.ts` is a per-op parameter or `settlement.claim.space` local (`1100, 6282, 6308`); the Provider/SpaceReplica receive only their own `space` (`open()` @1339-1376). The achievable discriminator exists and the scout already uses it: `read.space === this.#space` in the overlay path (`v2.ts:5856`) and `crossSpace` at the kick (`link-resolution.ts:311`). No fixture pins the cross-space kick's fire-every-read behavior (`grep docPullKick\|shouldPullDoc cross-space-value-read.test.ts` = 0), so a builder "making `#docPullKicks` reactivity-aware for foreign docs" would silently regress dangling/absent foreign-target retry semantics with the same-space suites unable to catch it. Note the class-label correction above (SpaceReplica, not Provider) — conclusion unaffected.

### [CONFIRMED / serious] V2 — WO-0 cannot pin the fix LAYER; B1-server is unowned
**WOs:** C3.12.0 (rescope acceptance + verdict); a new/contingent server-side WO or a gating clause on C3.12.1–.5. → **CR2**
**Reasoning:** Verified that every WO in the table is client-side and every WO-0 probe sits inside the reader's client session, downstream of the B-server→B-session boundary. A real B replica runs the standard standing-watch path with no cross-space guard (`open()` @1339 passes the shared `#subscription`; `refreshWatchSet` @3448+ registers `watchAddSync` + `consumeUpdates`), so "no standing watch registered" is false — which makes server-no-push / client-teardown / authority-swallow / dedup the live candidates. The `scheduler-observations`/`directDirtySeq` channel (`facade.ts:2192-2216`) is a **separate** cross-space push path from doc-value delivery (the fall-through at `facade.ts:2218`), so "the server propagates B→A" (gate header `:36`) does not establish that doc-*value* pushes reach the reader's B replica. WO-0 as written cannot separate "B's server emitted nothing" from "the client dropped/deduped it" — the exact axis that decides whether C3.12.1/.2 are the fix or a no-op.

### [CONFIRMED / serious] V3 — Owner-decision #1's rationale is factually backwards; standing-push's server surface understated
**WOs:** decision #1; C3.12.0 (extend B1 discriminant). → **CR3**
**Reasoning:** The core claim (rationale backwards) is confirmed against latch semantics (`v2.ts:1031-1037` "the first pull registers a server-side watch … a second kick is never needed") and the FB7 retract-on-eviction path (`v2.ts:1640-1645`): CT-1667 staleness is *never re-pulling*, and a wake-driven re-pull retracts the latch. The server "never pushes other-space docs" is confirmed at `link-resolution.ts:321-322`, so standing-push plausibly needs a delivering server-side foreign watch the table lacks. **Corrections:** the finding's `v2.ts:3519` "doc-set reconcile same-space-only" support is a misread (lane ≠ home-space) and must be dropped; the "wake-and-repull reuses the shipped `subscribeForeignWakes`" sub-claim is **plausible-not-proven** — `subscribeForeignWakes` (`executor/shared-execution-pool.ts`, `storage/v2-host-provider.ts`) is a host→read-host wake (the C3.3a *server* analog), so a *client* wake still needs a client→B-host channel. Neither correction touches the verdict: the stated rationale is wrong and the server-surface question is unrouted.

### [CONFIRMED / serious] V4 — C3.12.3 reuses fixtures that never exercise the client subscription; revocation is unowned and fail-open
**WOs:** C3.12.3 (mechanism + acceptance rewrite); sequencing note vs C3.12.2. → **CR4**

## Amendments

**CR1 (from V1) — Rewrite C3.12.1's mechanism and guard; propagate to C3.12.2/.3.**
(a) Drop `#docPullKicks` from the target latches — it is same-space-only (`shouldPullDoc`'s sole caller is `link-resolution.ts:332` under `!crossSpace`) and foreign docs bypass it; keep `#watchSelectorTracker` (`v2.ts:2546`, SpaceReplica) as the sole foreign re-fetch dedup to back with a standing subscription, plus the `#updatePromises`/`consumeUpdates(view.subscribeSync())` lifecycle (`refreshWatchSet`, `v2.ts:3498-3508`) on the foreign SpaceReplica. (b) Replace "strictly guarded `space !== homeSpace`" with a foreign-read signal threaded from the one seam that has the discriminator — `crossSpace` at `link-resolution.ts:311/333` — via a manager-owned keep-alive reservation (e.g. `keepForeignReadReplicaAlive(space,id,scope)`); the byte-identical guarantee then rests on "runs only for crossSpace-kick reservations," mirroring `read.space !== this.#space` at `v2.ts:5856`, not on a `homeSpace` test the layer cannot make (StorageManager has no `#homeSpace`; SpaceReplica knows only its own `#space`). (c) Key the eviction teardown off that reservation table, not the same-space `handleDocEviction`→`retractDocPullKick` path (`v2.ts:1640-1645`). (d) In C3.12.2/.3, replace the repeated "guarded on `space !== homeSpace`" at the delivery (`v2.ts:4985-4999`) and scheduler (`facade.ts:2179`) seams with "same-space byte-identical is preserved by NOT emitting foreign dependency edges for same-space reads; no `space !== homeSpace` guard is computable or needed there."

**CR2 (from V2) — Rescope WO-0 to a three-way verdict across the transport boundary.**
Add to WO-0's acceptance a session/transport-boundary probe (observe B's server watch-delivery / the reader's inbound B-session update stream for the third-party commit) and emit a three-way verdict: **{B2** = delivered, no reactive dirty; **B1-client** = delivered/deliverable to the session but replica torn-down / authority-swallowed / dedup-blocked re-pull; **B1-server** = origin emits no third-party push to the reader's B session**}**. Then either add one contingent row — **C3.12.1s** "server-side third-party push to a foreign read session" — or make C3.12.1–C3.12.5 explicitly conditional on WO-0 returning a client-side verdict, so a B1-server outcome is not left unowned. Fold in the discriminant from CR3.

**CR3 (from V3) — Correct decision #1's rationale and route the server-delivery question into WO-0.**
Rewrite decision #1 to weigh the real trade-off: standing-push (lower latency; but requires a delivering server-side foreign watch that pushes third-party B commits — new server surface the table lacks per `link-resolution.ts:321-322` — plus per-`(client,space)` push state) vs wake-and-repull (a re-pull round-trip that explicitly retracts the pull-kick latch, so it does **not** reintroduce CT-1667 staleness; client wake channel still to be built). Delete the `v2.ts:3519` "doc-set reconcile same-space-only" support (misread) and downgrade "reuses `subscribeForeignWakes`" to "reuses the wake *pattern*, client channel TBD." Add to WO-0's B1 discriminant (CR2): **does the reader's foreign watch actually receive third-party B commits** — if not, standing-push needs a server-side leg and wake-and-repull is the lower-surface path.

**CR4 (from V4) — Give C3.12.3 a real client-subscription revocation edge and fixture.**
(1) Add a new client-subscription revocation edge — a home-replica epoch-bump / B-READ-retraction observer that tears down the standing foreign-read subscription and evicts the foreign replica (handing back CR1's keep-alive reservation) **independently of the claim lifecycle**, re-checking READ on each subscription-driven recompute (fail-closed, not the claim's fail-open-via-`dirtyProducer`-rerun at `v2.ts:6136-6142`). (2) Require a new runner/integration fixture in C3.12.0's two-runtime `StorageManager` shape (retract B-READ mid-subscription → delivery stops, no further recompute, held overlay dropped, fail-closed) as C3.12.3's acceptance; reclassify `v2-execution-cross-space-{epoch,idle-revocation}-test.ts` (`packages/memory/test/`) as the authority **source** (epoch table + claim revoke), not the acceptance vehicle, and state they cannot go green on the client hole.
